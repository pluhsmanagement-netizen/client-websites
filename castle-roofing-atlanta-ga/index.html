FAQ

Common questions, straightforward answers.

How do I know if my Atlanta roof needs replacement or just a repair?

If your roof is under 15 years old and damage is localized, a targeted repair is often sufficient. However, widespread granule loss, multiple leaks, sagging decking, or a roof older than 20-25 years typically warrants a full replacement. We offer free inspections to give you an honest assessment.

How long does a full roof replacement take in Atlanta?

Most residential roof replacements in Atlanta can be completed in one to two days, depending on roof size and complexity. We schedule around Atlanta's weather patterns, communicate the project timeline clearly before we begin, and work efficiently to minimize disruption to your household.

Does Castle Roofing help with insurance claims after storm damage?

Yes. We have extensive experience working alongside Atlanta homeowners and their insurance adjusters to document storm damage accurately and facilitate claims. Our team provides detailed photo reports and material assessments to support your claim from start to finish.

What roofing materials do you recommend for Atlanta's climate?

Architectural asphalt shingles are the most popular and cost-effective option for Atlanta's hot summers and occasional severe storms. For premium durability and energy efficiency, standing seam metal roofing is an excellent long-term investment. We'll help you weigh options based on your budget and home style during your free estimate.

Are you available for emergency roofing services in Atlanta?

Absolutely. Castle Roofing operates 24/7 across Greater Atlanta for emergency tarping and storm damage response. If severe weather has compromised your roof's integrity, call us immediately at (470) 601-6422 and we'll dispatch a crew to protect your home as quickly as possible.

What warranty do you offer on roofing work?

We provide a labor warranty on all installations and repairs, and manufacturer warranties apply to the materials we install. Warranty specifics vary by material and project scope — we outline all warranty terms clearly in your written proposal before any work begins, so you know exactly what's covered.
